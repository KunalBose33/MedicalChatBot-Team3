On the day of the abortion, drugs, usually prosta- glandins to induce contractions, and a salt water solution, are injected into the uterus. Contractions begin, and with- in eight to 72 hours the woman delivers the fetus. Side effects of this procedure include nausea, vomit- ing, and diarrhea from the prostaglandins, and pain from uterine cramps. Anesthesia of the sort used in childbirth can be given to mask the pain. Many women are able to go home a few hours after the procedure. Very early abortions cost between $200-$400. Later abortions cost more. The cost increases about $100 per week between the thirteenth and sixteenth week. Second trimester abortions are much more costly because they often involve more risk, more services, anesthesia, and sometimes a hospital stay. Insurance carriers and HMOs may or may not cover the procedure.
